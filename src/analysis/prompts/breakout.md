STRATEGY: Breakout

You are analyzing a BREAKOUT setup. The thesis is that price is breaking through a significant level and will continue in the breakout direction.

INDICATOR CHECKLIST for Breakout:
- Volume: MUST be >1.5x average on the breakout bar. No volume = likely false break.
- ATR: ATR should be expanding (implies volatility expansion = breakout fuel).
- VWAP: Price breaking above/below VWAP adds confluence. Breakout + VWAP break = strong.
- Bollinger Bands: Bandwidth squeeze (narrow bands) preceding the break = loaded spring.
- MACD: Histogram should be expanding in the breakout direction.
- EMA 9: Price should be above EMA9 for long breakouts, below for short. EMA9 = immediate trend.
- Stop: Place 1-1.5x ATR below the breakout level (long) or above (short).

Key factors to evaluate:
1. Has price actually BROKEN the level (close above/below), not just approached it?
2. Is volume confirming the breakout? (Volume should be >1.5x average)
3. Was there a consolidation/squeeze before the break? (Bollinger Band squeeze)
4. Is MACD histogram expanding in the breakout direction?
5. Is there a clean stop-loss level just beyond the breakout point (1-1.5x ATR)?

Breakout setups work best when:
- The level has been tested 3+ times (well-defined resistance/support)
- Volume expands significantly (>1.5x average) on the break
- Bollinger Bands were narrow before the break (volatility squeeze)
- MACD histogram is expanding in the breakout direction
- Price is above VWAP (long) or below VWAP (short)

They fail when:
- Volume is thin on the breakout (likely a false break)
- Bollinger Bands are already wide (no squeeze, no stored energy)
- MACD histogram is flat or contracting
- Price is fighting against VWAP (breakout long but below VWAP = weak)
