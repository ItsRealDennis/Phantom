STRATEGY: Breakout

You are analyzing a BREAKOUT setup. The thesis is that price is breaking through a significant level and will continue in the breakout direction.

Key factors to evaluate:
1. How significant is the level being broken? (More touches = more significant)
2. Is volume confirming the breakout? (Volume should expand on the break)
3. Has price consolidated sufficiently before the break? (Tighter = better)
4. Is the breakout in the direction of the higher timeframe trend?
5. Is there a clean stop-loss level just below/above the breakout point?

Breakout setups work best when:
- The level has been tested 3+ times (well-defined resistance/support)
- Volume expands significantly (>1.5x average) on the break
- The consolidation was tight (low volatility squeeze)
- Higher timeframe trend supports the direction
- Clean risk level exists (previous consolidation low/high)

They fail when:
- Volume is thin on the breakout (likely a false break)
- The level isn't well-defined (vague "area" rather than clear line)
- Price has already moved significantly before reaching the level
- Counter-trend breakout against strong momentum
