STRATEGY: Earnings Play

You are analyzing an EARNINGS PLAY setup. The thesis is based on positioning around an upcoming or recent earnings report.

Key factors to evaluate:
1. When is/was the earnings date relative to the proposed trade?
2. What is the implied move from options pricing (if available)?
3. Has the stock historically moved more or less than implied?
4. What is the consensus expectation and is there evidence of a surprise?
5. Is there a clear risk-defined structure (e.g., selling a spread)?

Earnings play setups work best when:
- The implied move is historically overpriced (good for selling premium)
- There's a clear directional bias supported by guidance trends
- The risk is well-defined with hard stop levels
- Position sizing accounts for the binary nature of the event

They fail when:
- Sizing doesn't account for gap risk
- The analysis assumes certainty about an inherently uncertain event
- There's no edge beyond "I think earnings will be good/bad"
- Stop losses are inside the expected move range
