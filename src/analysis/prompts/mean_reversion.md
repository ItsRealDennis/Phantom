STRATEGY: Mean Reversion

You are analyzing a MEAN REVERSION setup. The thesis is that price has deviated significantly from its mean and is likely to revert.

INDICATOR CHECKLIST for Mean Reversion:
- RSI: Should be >70 (short setup) or <30 (long setup). 40-60 = no edge, skip it.
- Bollinger %B: Should be >1.0 (above upper band, short) or <0.0 (below lower band, long).
- VWAP: Price should be significantly away from VWAP (>0.5%). Reversion target is often VWAP itself.
- MACD Histogram: Look for histogram SHRINKING (momentum fading). Don't fight accelerating momentum.
- Volume: Should be declining on the extended move (exhaustion), not expanding.
- ATR: Use 1.5-2x ATR for stop loss. Target = move back toward SMA20 or VWAP.

Key factors to evaluate:
1. How far has price deviated from the 20-period and 50-period moving averages?
2. Is RSI at an extreme (>70 or <30)? Without RSI confirmation, confidence should be LOW.
3. Is there evidence of exhaustion (volume declining, long wicks, MACD histogram shrinking)?
4. Is price at or beyond Bollinger Bands? %B > 1.0 or < 0.0 = strong reversion signal.
5. Where is VWAP relative to price? VWAP often acts as a reversion magnet intraday.

Mean reversion setups work best when:
- RSI is at an extreme AND price is beyond Bollinger Bands (double confirmation)
- Volume is declining on the extended move (exhaustion)
- MACD histogram is shrinking (momentum fading)
- There's a clear level to anchor the reversal (VWAP, SMA20, swing high/low)

They fail when:
- A fundamental catalyst justifies the new price level
- MACD histogram is EXPANDING (momentum accelerating, not fading)
- Volume is INCREASING on the move (trend, not mean reversion)
- RSI is in neutral territory (40-60) — no actual overextension
