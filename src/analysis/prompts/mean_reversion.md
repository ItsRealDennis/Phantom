STRATEGY: Mean Reversion

You are analyzing a MEAN REVERSION setup. The thesis is that price has deviated significantly from its mean and is likely to revert.

Key factors to evaluate:
1. How far has price deviated from the 20-period and 50-period moving averages?
2. Is there evidence of exhaustion (volume climax, long wicks, RSI extreme)?
3. Is there a clear support/resistance level where price is likely to reverse?
4. Is the broader trend still intact, or has the trend broken?
5. Are there any catalysts (earnings, macro events) that justify the move continuing?

Mean reversion setups work best when:
- Price is at a historically significant standard deviation from the mean
- Volume is declining on the extended move (exhaustion)
- There's a clear level to anchor the reversal
- The broader market isn't trending hard against the reversal direction

They fail when:
- A fundamental catalyst justifies the new price level
- The move is part of a larger trend break
- Volume is INCREASING on the move (momentum, not mean reversion)
