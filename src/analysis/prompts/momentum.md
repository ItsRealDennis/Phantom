STRATEGY: Momentum

You are analyzing a MOMENTUM setup. The thesis is that a strong move is underway and will continue in the current direction.

INDICATOR CHECKLIST for Momentum:
- MACD Histogram: Must be positive (long) or negative (short) and preferably EXPANDING.
- RSI: Should be 50-70 for longs, 30-50 for shorts. Avoid >75 (too extended) or <25 (too extended).
- EMA 9: Price should be above EMA9 (long) or below (short). EMA9 slope confirms trend.
- VWAP: For continuation, price should be above VWAP (long) or below VWAP (short).
- Volume: Should be stable or increasing with the trend, not diverging.
- ATR: Use 1-1.5x ATR for stop. Tighter stop on pullback entries, wider on momentum entries.
- Entry: Ideal on pullback to EMA9 or VWAP, not at extension from both.

Key factors to evaluate:
1. Is MACD histogram expanding (momentum accelerating) or contracting (fading)?
2. Is RSI in the sweet spot (50-70 for longs)? Not at extremes that suggest exhaustion.
3. Is price above EMA9 with EMA9 slope rising (long) or below with slope falling (short)?
4. Is volume supporting the trend (stable or increasing)?
5. Is this a pullback entry to a support level (EMA9, VWAP) or a chase entry at extension?

Momentum setups work best when:
- MACD histogram is positive and expanding (longs) or negative and expanding (shorts)
- RSI is in the 50-70 zone (strong but not exhausted)
- Price pulls back to EMA9 or VWAP and holds (buy the dip in an uptrend)
- Volume confirms the trend direction
- Entry is at a support level, not at the top of a move

They fail when:
- MACD histogram is contracting (momentum fading even if price is up)
- RSI is >75 or <25 (overextended, reversal risk)
- Volume is diverging (price up, volume down = no real buying)
- Entering at resistance without a pullback (chasing)
- Price is far from EMA9 and VWAP (overextended entry)
