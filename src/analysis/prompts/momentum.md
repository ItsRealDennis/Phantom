STRATEGY: Momentum

You are analyzing a MOMENTUM setup. The thesis is that a strong move is underway and will continue in the current direction.

Key factors to evaluate:
1. How strong is the current trend? (Consecutive candles, angle of move)
2. Is volume supporting the trend? (Increasing or at least stable)
3. Has there been a pullback to a logical entry point? (Buying the dip in an uptrend)
4. Are there upcoming catalysts that could accelerate or reverse the move?
5. How extended is the move already? (Late entries have worse R:R)

Momentum setups work best when:
- The trend is clear and strong (higher highs/higher lows or vice versa)
- Volume is expanding with the trend
- Entry is on a pullback to a moving average or prior breakout level
- Sector/market context supports the direction
- The move is relatively early (not already extended 5+ days without a pullback)

They fail when:
- The move is already late-stage and extended
- Volume is diverging (price up, volume down = exhaustion)
- Entering at resistance without a pullback
- Counter to sector/market trend
- Major catalyst approaching (earnings, FOMC) that creates binary risk
